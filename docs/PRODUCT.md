# PRODUCT.md — Algoplay

## Register
product

## Users
Computer science students (18-25) learning algorithms for the first time. They study on their phones between classes, on the bus, in bright campus libraries, and late at night in dorm rooms. They need clarity over cleverness. They're anxious about interviews and want to feel progress quickly.

## Product Purpose
Algoplay teaches data structures and algorithms through interactive visualizations, gamified challenges, and an AI tutor. Users watch algorithms run step-by-step, play mini-games that test their understanding, and build mastery through XP, streaks, and a skill tree.

## Brand Personality
Friendly, warm, encouraging. Like a patient tutor who believes in you. Not edgy, not cyberpunk, not intimidating. The app says "you've got this" not "prove yourself." Solar energy: bright, warm, optimistic.

## Tone
Clear and direct. No jargon without explanation. Celebrate small wins. Use color to guide attention, never to decorate.

## Anti-References
- Dark mode-first educational apps (Duolingo uses bright colors for a reason)
- Cyberpunk/hacker aesthetics (the old React app used CyberBackground with neon particles — drop this)
- intimidating code-editor UIs
- Pastel everything (too soft, no energy)
- Rainbow chaos (each element a different color)

## Strategic Principles
1. **White is the canvas.** Clean white surfaces let the solar accent colors pop. The eye goes straight to what matters.
2. **Color = meaning.** Blue for primary actions. Orange for energy/achievements. Green for success. Red for errors only. Purple for exploration/discovery.
3. **Motion serves understanding.** Animations show algorithm steps, not decorate empty space. A sorting bar swapping positions teaches; a pulsing button distracts.
4. **Progress feels warm.** XP gains, streaks, level-ups use warm solar colors (amber, orange, gold) to feel rewarding.
5. **One thing at a time.** Each screen has one clear purpose. No visual clutter competing for attention.
